Energy Sources

A Guide to Renewable Energy Sources

Solar Energy

Solar energy is radiant light and heat from the Sun that is harnessed using a range of ever-evolving technologies such as solar heating, photovoltaics, solar thermal energy, solar architecture, molten salt power plants and artificial photosynthesis. Solar energy is everywhere. It is in plants. It is inside us. Now we can use solar energy is in a new way ,creating energy. It is harvested by using photovoltaic panels to convert the solar energy into electrical energy. This proves to be a much better way of using it as most of the devices we use cannot work directly on the heat component of solar energy but need electricity to run.

Wind Energy

We have been using wind energy for a long time. We used it to mill grain through windmills. Wind energy has been essential for life since 5 A.D. Wind energy in its modern form however is a little more recent. Basically wind energy is converted to electricity when a turbine with usually two to three blades is turned. That force runs a generator which then can produce electricity.

Geothermal

Geothermal energy is the heat from the Earth. It's clean and sustainable. Resources of geothermal energy range from the shallow ground to hot water and hot rock found a few miles beneath the Earth's surface, and down even deeper to the extremely high temperatures of molten rock called. Geothermal energy is harvested by the steam from the heat turning a turbine and then that turbine runs a generator.

    Biomass

    What is Biomass? Well it is basically organic material. This includes wood, crops ,and garbage. If you think about it we have used these for hundreds of years. These include Heating houses with fireplaces and Making food with fire by burning wood . Only in the past few decades did we realize we could use this to create electricity. Now I can tell you how it works. Biomass is Burned with a massive Boiler. Then the heat from it creates steam. That steam moves through massive pipes. Those pipes go to a turbine and the power of the steam moves it. That turbine runs a generator that creates electricity.
